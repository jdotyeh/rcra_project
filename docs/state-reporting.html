KSQGs, SQGs, and LQGs re-notify the KDHE each year to confirm generator status, and SQGs additionally re-notify the EPA every 4 years (2021, 2025).No state-specific waste subject to RCRA manifest and reporting requirements.Kansas Department of Health and Environment · Encamp

MaineUses RCRAInfo, Paper Copy acceptedMaine uses three generator categories based on waste generated or accumulated, namely SQG, SQG Plus, and LQG. LQGs are required to report, filing federal biennial reports through RCRAInfo in even-numbered years while the DEP sends an annual reporting packet in odd-numbered years. SQGs and SQG Plus generators are exempt from reporting. Generators re-notify the EPA every 4 years by submitting form 8700-12 by September 1 (2021, 2025). Maine also assigns a generic EPA identification number for use on manifests.Maine regulates additional wastes beyond the federal list, including M002 for PCBs at any concentration and additional K, P, and U-listed wastes.Maine Department of Environmental Protection · Encamp
MarylandUses RCRAInfo, Paper Copy acceptedFully regulated generators and TSDFs are required to submit biennial reports by March 1 of every even-numbered year, covering the prior odd year. Maryland Small Quantity Generators (MDSQGs) are exempt from reporting. Facilities re-notify MDE every 4 years using EPA Form 8700-12 through myRCRAid. Because the fully regulated category takes in federally defined SQGs, every federal SQG in Maryland files a biennial report.Maryland regulates state-listed wastes including chemical warfare agent codes (K991 through K998, MD01 through MD03) and PCB-containing wastes (M001, MT01, MX01), folding much of what would be TSCA-regulated material into its hazardous waste program.Maryland Department of the Environment · Encamp

Same as federal definitions.Massachusetts designates state waste codes that count toward generator status, including MA02 for PCB wastes over 50 ppm, MA04 for paint manufacturing waste, and MA01 and MA98 for waste oil and off-specification used oil fuel.Massachusetts Department of Environmental Protection · Encamp

Same as federal definitions.Michigan lists state-specific hazardous waste codes (001S through 007S) for dioxins and furans, and separately regulates Liquid Industrial By-products such as antifreeze, leachate, and used oil.Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy · Encamp
MinnesotaUses RCRAInfo, Paper Copy acceptedGenerators are required to submit an annual Hazardous Waste Generator License Application to the MPCA or their metropolitan county between January 1 and August 15, reporting all waste generated in the prior year and paying a fee. Generators obtain their handler ID through the MPCA's online e-Services rather than EPA Form 8700-12.Minnesota regulates state-listed hazardous wastes with codes such as MN01 through MN07, and some metropolitan counties add their own local waste codes.Minnesota Pollution Control Agency · Encamp

Same as federal definitions.No state-specific waste subject to RCRA manifest and reporting requirements.Mississippi Department of Environmental Quality · Encamp
