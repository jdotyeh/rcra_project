KansasUses RCRAInfoPaper Copy accepted

Classify into CESQG, KSQG, SQG, and LQG:

  • CESQG (Conditionally Exempt Small): ≤25 kg of HW and ≤1 kg acutely HW
  • KSQG (Kansas-Defined Small): ≥25 kg and <100 kg HW, and ≤1 kg acutely HW
  • SQGs and LQGs are the same as federal definitions

KSQGs, SQGs, and LQGs are required to re-notify the KDHE every year confirming generator status.

SQGs are further required to re-notify the EPA every 4 years (2021, 2025).

MaineUses RCRAInfoPaper Copy accepted

LQGs are required to submit annual reports to the DEP.

Maine uses three generator categories based on waste generated or accumulated: SQG, SQG Plus, and LQG.

  • SQG: ≤100 kg of HW and ≤1 kg of acutely HW
  • SQG Plus: different accumulation threshold
  • LQG: same as federal definitions

SQG Plus generators are required to re-notify the EPA every 4 years (2021, 2025).

Note: generic EPA ID number in manifests.

State-specific waste code groups:
  • Additional waste codes specific to Maine, identified by M (M002: PCBs at 50 ppm or greater)
  • Expansion of federal waste groups to include additional K, P, and U codes

MarylandUses RCRAInfoPaper Copy accepted

Classify into MDSQG (Maryland-Defined Small Quantity Generators) and FRG (Fully Regulated Generators):

  • MDSQG: ≤100 kg HW and ≤1 kg acutely HW
  • FRG: ≥100 kg HW and ≥1 kg acutely HW

FRGs are required to re-notify the EPA every 4 years.

Note that FRG includes both SQGs and LQGs, meaning all federally defined SQGs in Maryland are required to submit biennial reports.

State-specific waste codes:
  • K991, K992, K993, K994, K995, K996, K997, K998, K999, MD01, MD02, MD03, M001, MT01, MX01
Federal codes remaining in Maryland’s regulations:

MinnesotaUses RCRAInfoPaper Copy accepted

State-specific waste codes:
